South Africa — 30 June Anti-Immigration Shutdown Monitoring
As of 29 June 2026  ·  Sources: S&P Global / IHS Markit  ·  Sentinel SecureWatch  ·  Peer Network  ·  Contact Assessments (WM, ABM, JM)
Key Insights
Current Situation
  • •Anti-immigration mobilization has escalated ahead of the 30 June deadline, led by Operation Dudula and "March and March"; three deaths now confirmed linked to anti-migrant violence; a coordinated nationwide shutdown remains unlikely but widespread localized disruption on 30 June is near-certain.
  • •Gauteng now officially rated moderate-to-high risk with confirmed mobilization in Tembisa, Jeppestown, and City Deep corridor; KZN remains the primary flashpoint with eThekwini refusing March and March permission to march in Durban on national security grounds.
  • •R600 million redirected within SAPS for operational readiness; all police leave cancelled for 30 June; army on standby to protect critical infrastructure; private security companies on high alert.
Key Implications
  • •Widespread localized disruption on 30 June is near-certain; primary impact is movement disruption — road blockages, public transport interruption, restricted site access — rather than direct threat to corporate environments; government repeatedly stressing 30 June is a normal business day.
  • •KZN and Durban remain the major hotspots; confirmed march routes now published — Durban (King Dinuzulu Park, 10am) and Pretoria (Church Square, 10am to Sunnyside Police Station, dispersing ~15:00); eThekwini's refusal to grant march permission may itself become a flashpoint.
  • •Within Gauteng, the East Rand industrial corridor and City Deep logistics corridor carry the highest exposure; contacts assess mainstream Gauteng and Sandton as likely to be relatively quiet, with incidents concentrated in informal settlements; Centre for Risk Analysis assesses most likely outcome as localised friction with sporadic violence rather than widespread unrest.
  • •Social media misinformation is an active risk multiplier — government has engaged social media platforms over manipulated content; verify all reports before acting.

Anti-Foreigner Sentiments & Threats
 Elevated ↘ Deteriorating Anti-foreigner mobilization has intensified ahead of 30 June, with three deaths now confirmed linked to anti-migrant violence; incidents extend beyond undocumented migrants to legal foreign nationals and foreign-owned businesses, with vigilante groups expected to treat the deadline as authorization for further direct action.
👁 What to watch▲
Escalation triggers. Post-deadline vigilante action is the key medium-term risk — fringe groups are likely to treat midnight 30 June as authorization for direct action against foreign nationals still in the country, meaning risk may peak in days after rather than on 30 June itself. Monitor transport union posture (All-Truck Drivers Forum, SANTACO, ARDF-SA) — if 30 June produces no satisfactory government response, escalation into freight and logistics sector is likely. Retaliatory attacks on South African businesses in Nigeria remain a live risk.
Civil Unrest & Disruption — Johannesburg & Major Cities
 Elevated ↘ Deteriorating Large marches confirmed for 30 June across major metros; march routes now published for Durban (King Dinuzulu Park, 10am) and Pretoria (Church Square, 10am); eThekwini has refused March and March permission to march in Durban on national security grounds, which may itself become a flashpoint; road closures and CBD delays are expected.
👁 What to watch▲
Durban flashpoint. eThekwini has refused March and March permission to march — if they proceed anyway, direct confrontation with police is likely and could escalate quickly. MK party's confirmed participation in KZN significantly raises crowd size and intensity risk in the province. Monitor social media carefully — government has engaged platforms directly over manipulated videos and images designed to incite violence; the misinformation environment is more organized and deliberate than typical protest rumour-spreading.
Civil Unrest & Disruption — Gauteng
 Elevated ↘ Deteriorating Gauteng is now officially rated moderate-to-high risk, with confirmed mobilization in Tembisa, Jeppestown, and the City Deep market corridor; SAPS and private security are deployed province-wide, with the army on standby to protect critical infrastructure.
👁 What to watch▲
City Deep and East Rand corridor. Confirmed mobilization in Tembisa, Jeppestown, and City Deep market corridor — organizations with logistics routes through these areas should confirm alternative routing for 30 June. Monitor freight and logistics sector closely post-30 June; if transport unions escalate, City Deep and Ekurhuleni industrial corridor face significantly higher disruption risk.
